### Symptom
Evaluating SPLADE models for a hybrid RAG pipeline. Most tutorials and benchmarks reference `naver/splade-cocondenser-ensembledistil` as the standard SPLADE model.

### Context
Selecting a SPLADE model for sparse embeddings in a commercial platform (casehub-neural-text). The naver model is the most-cited in blog posts, Pinecone docs, and academic papers.

### Root cause
`naver/splade-cocondenser-ensembledistil` is released under Creative Commons NonCommercial. The license is not prominent on the HuggingFace model card — you discover it by checking the repo's license field, not from the README or usage examples.

### Fix
Use `prithivida/Splade_PP_en_v1` instead. It achieves equivalent MRR@10 (~37.22 vs ~37.2-38.3 for the naver model) with lower FLOPS and ~10% fewer tokens. It initialises from vanilla `bert-base-uncased` rather than CoCondenser pre-trained weights, and is released under a more permissive license.

ONNX export available at `onnx-models/Splade_PP_en_v1-onnx` on HuggingFace (~436MB).

### Why this is non-obvious
The "official" model from the paper authors is the natural first choice. Tutorials don't mention the license. The permissive alternative (`Splade_PP_en_v1`) is an independent reimplementation — not obvious that it achieves equivalent quality without checking benchmarks directly.
