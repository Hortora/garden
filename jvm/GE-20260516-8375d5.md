### What happened

Adding `casehub-engine` as a test dependency caused Quarkus to index all 30+ internal
CDI beans from the engine jar — `CaseHubRuntimeImpl`, `CaseContextChangedEventHandler`,
`WorkflowExecutionCompletedHandler`, etc. — even though the engine was intended as a
plain library. This caused CDI ambiguity errors and required explicit exclusion of each
bean in `quarkus.arc.exclude-types`.

### Root cause

Quarkus's augmentation step detects "application archives" by looking for
`application.properties` (or `application.yaml`) at the root of a jar. If found,
the jar is treated as an application and all its classes are scanned for CDI beans,
REST endpoints, and other Quarkus extension hooks — identical to how the main application
module is processed. `casehub-engine` ships with its own `application.properties` for
its Quarkus extension configuration, which triggers this behaviour when the jar appears
on the test classpath.

### Fix

Option 1 — Exclude the specific beans you don't want in `quarkus.arc.exclude-types`:
```properties
%test.quarkus.arc.exclude-types=\
  io.casehub.engine.internal.engine.CaseHubRuntimeImpl,\
  io.casehub.engine.internal.engine.handler.CaseContextChangedEventHandler,...
```

Option 2 — If you control the library: strip `application.properties` from the jar's
root in its Maven build, keeping it only on the compile classpath (not packaged).

Option 3 — Use `quarkus.index-dependency` exclusions to prevent the jar from being
Jandex-indexed at all (prevents CDI scan entirely, but also prevents type-safe injection
of any classes from that jar).

### Why this is non-obvious

Library jars are normally inert — they don't activate Quarkus scanning unless explicitly
indexed. The `application.properties` heuristic is undocumented as a trigger for full
application-archive treatment. A developer adding a Quarkus extension jar as a test dep
has no reason to expect its internal beans to become active CDI candidates.
