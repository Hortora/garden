### Symptom

A replay validation harness comparing a continuously-simulated mineral balance against ground-truth (GT) replay data shows large apparent divergence. Attempts to correct the divergence by deducting building costs from the simulated balance at the tick a building appears in the GT snapshot make the divergence significantly worse — unit-count delta goes from 2 to 48+.

### Context

Implementing a replay validation harness that replays SC2 tracker events into a simulated game engine and compares simulated state against GT observations tick-by-tick (22-loop outer ticks at Faster speed).

### What was tried

Deducting building mineral costs from the simulated mineral balance at the outer tick when the building first appears in `gt.myBuildings()`. The GT mineral reading at that tick was used as a reference for whether the simulated balance was reasonable.

### Root cause

SC2 `PlayerStats` events in replays fire approximately every 10 real-game-time seconds (~224 game loops, ~10 outer ticks at Faster speed) — not at every 22-loop tick boundary. The `gt.minerals()` reading is stale: it reflects the player's mineral balance from the previous `PlayerStats` event, potentially 10 ticks behind reality.

When a building is ordered during tick 15, the GT mineral reading may still show the value from tick 5. The real player had ~10 ticks of additional income beyond what the GT reading reflects. Deducting the building cost against the simulated balance (which is continuously tracked and lower than the real player's actual balance at that moment) pushes the simulated balance into artificial debt, blocking subsequent training.

### Fix

Do not use stale GT mineral readings to calibrate or deduct against a continuously-tracked simulated balance. Two alternatives:

1. **Sync minerals from GT at each tick** before applying train intents — gives exact train-timing parity at the cost of mineral divergence tracking.
2. **Track building costs independently** of GT mineral readings — deduct building costs from a separate running total rather than trying to align with stale GT snapshots.

Verify the sampling interval by printing `gt.minerals()` across successive ticks and looking for flat segments — stale readings appear as the same value repeating for 8–12 consecutive ticks.

### Why this is non-obvious

The SC2 `PlayerStats` event frequency is documented in the API (fires every 10 game-time seconds) but is rarely considered in replay analysis code. The staleness is ~10 ticks — long enough to cause false divergence signals but short enough to look like model noise. The symptom (apparent model error in mineral tracking) points directly away from the cause (sampling rate mismatch).
