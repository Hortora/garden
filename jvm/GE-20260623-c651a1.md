### Symptom

After removing `@Alternative @Priority(2)` from one CDI bean, a different bean
silently becomes the active implementation. No `AmbiguousResolutionException`, no
warning, no log. Tests that depend on the removed bean's behaviour fail with
unrelated-looking assertion errors.

### Context

Three implementations of the same port interface coexist in the build:

| Bean | Annotations | Role |
|------|------------|------|
| `PrReviewService` | `@ApplicationScoped @DefaultBean` | Layer 1 naive baseline |
| `QhorusPrReviewService` | `@ApplicationScoped @Alternative @Priority(1)` | Layer 3 qhorus messaging |
| `PrReviewCaseService` | `@ApplicationScoped @Alternative @Priority(2)` | Layer 5 engine (active) |

This is the CDI displacement pattern for progressive layer integration — each layer
adds an implementation; CDI priority determines which is active at runtime.

### What was tried (didn't work)

Removed `@Alternative @Priority(2)` from `PrReviewCaseService` to fix an anti-pattern
documented in ARC42STORIES §8 ("CDI priority gymnastics"). Expected: the plain
`@ApplicationScoped` bean would still displace the `@DefaultBean` baseline via
Quarkus's `@DefaultBean` semantics.

### Root cause

CDI resolution rules interact in a non-obvious way:

1. `@DefaultBean` beans are suppressed when any non-`@DefaultBean` bean exists ✅
2. Activated `@Alternative` beans (with `@Priority`) take precedence over regular beans ⚠️
3. After removing `@Alternative` from `PrReviewCaseService`, it becomes a regular bean
4. `QhorusPrReviewService` (`@Alternative @Priority(1)`) is still an activated alternative
5. **Rule 2 applies**: the activated alternative wins over the regular bean
6. `QhorusPrReviewService` becomes the active implementation — silently

The mental model "I'm just removing unnecessary annotations" is wrong. The annotations
are load-bearing: they participate in a priority chain where every bean's annotation
set matters.

### Fix

All implementations in the `@Alternative` chain must be addressed together, not one at
a time. To properly fix the anti-pattern:

- Either make ALL non-baseline implementations use `@DefaultBean` displacement
  (remove `@Alternative @Priority` from all of them and ensure only one non-`@DefaultBean` exists)
- Or keep the `@Alternative @Priority` chain intact and accept it as the mechanism
  for layer coexistence

Do not remove `@Alternative` from one bean without checking what other `@Alternative`
implementations exist for the same interface.

### Why this is non-obvious

Most developers reason about CDI alternatives in pairs: "my bean vs the default."
The three-way interaction (DefaultBean + Alternative(1) + Alternative(2)) is rarely
encountered, and the silent resolution change (no exception, wrong bean) gives no
diagnostic signal. The symptom (test assertion failure on unrelated data) is
completely disconnected from the cause (CDI annotation change).

### Why this fix

The obvious approach — "just remove the anti-pattern annotation" — is wrong because
it treats the annotations as decoration rather than as a load-bearing priority chain.
The fix requires understanding the full set of implementations and their annotation
interactions before changing any single one.

### Alternatives considered

- Remove `@Alternative @Priority(2)` from only the active bean → silently breaks resolution (this gotcha)
- Remove `@Alternative` from ALL implementations and use `@DefaultBean` displacement → requires ensuring exactly one non-`@DefaultBean` impl, and retiring or `@DefaultBean`-ing the intermediate layers
- Keep `@Alternative @Priority` chain as-is → accepted as the working mechanism until a coordinated cleanup addresses all implementations together
