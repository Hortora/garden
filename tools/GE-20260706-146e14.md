### Symptom
Retrieval benchmark shows low precision (50%) on certain scenarios. Failure mode labelled "DOMAIN_ABSENCE" — entries for the topic don't exist in the corpus.

### Context
Running a 14-scenario retrieval benchmark comparing gardenSearch (hybrid dense+sparse+BM25+ColBERT) against grep. Some scenarios showed gardenSearch at 50% precision while grep scored 100%. Initial diagnosis: the corpus lacks entries for those topics.

### What was tried (didn't work)
- Accepted the DOMAIN_ABSENCE label from the original benchmark (#27)
- Investigated corpus coverage gaps as the primary bottleneck
- Considered corpus curation and new entry creation as the fix

### Root cause
The entries DO exist — grep finds them with 100% precision. gardenSearch also finds them, but buries them in noise (irrelevant results pushing relevant entries out of the top-K window). The actual failure mode is POLYSEMY (generic terms like "scan", "pagination" matching many unrelated entries) or VOCABULARY_GAP (query terms not overlapping with entry vocabulary).

### Fix
Before labelling any benchmark scenario as "DOMAIN_ABSENCE", run the same query through grep. If grep finds relevant entries, the failure is ranking precision, not corpus coverage. The entries exist — the retrieval system just isn't surfacing them.

### Why this is non-obvious
The diagnosis "entries don't exist" is logical when precision is low — it's the simplest explanation. The non-obvious part is that low precision can mean "entries exist but are buried in noise" rather than "entries are missing". The two failure modes have completely different fixes: corpus curation vs ranking improvement. Misdiagnosis wastes effort on the wrong track.
